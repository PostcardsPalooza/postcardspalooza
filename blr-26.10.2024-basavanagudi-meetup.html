Postcrossing Meetup at the Indian Institute of World Culture: A Philatelic Delight

On a remarkable day, Postcrossing enthusiasts gathered at the Indian Institute of World Culture to celebrate their passion for philately. The event coincided with the captivating postage stamp exhibit, "170 NOT OUT," curated by Inddak, a renowned philatelic publication. This meetup became a vibrant confluence of history, art, and connection, resonating with every attendee.

The Exhibit: "170 NOT OUT" by Inddak

The "170 NOT OUT" exhibit commemorates 170 years of postage stamp usage in India. This exhibit pays homage to the rich history and evolution of philately in India.

A Glimpse into the Collection

The collection includes a variety of rare and significant stamps:

  • The Penny Black: The world’s first commercially issued stamp.
  • Freedom Fighters Series: Honoring figures like Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Bhagat Singh, Vinayak Damodar Savarkar (Veer Savarkar), and Mahatma Gandhi.
  • Thematic Stamps: Showcasing diverse themes, from animals and paintings to celebrated movie personalities like Raj Kumar, Madhubala, and M S Subbulakshmi.
  • Historical Milestones: British service stamps, the first stamps issued after India’s independence, and many others.

The First Postage Stamp of India

India’s philatelic journey began with the issuance of the 1/2 Anna Blue stamp on October 1, 1854, depicting Queen Victoria. This stamp was designed by Captain Thuillier and engraved by the talented Muniruddin at the Surveyor General's office in Calcutta.

Pioneers of Philately

Sir Henry Edward Landor Thuillier

  • Surveyor General of India, responsible for printing India’s first postage stamps.
  • Surveyed vast regions of India and contributed significantly to its cartographic development.
  • Recognized with prestigious honors such as CSI (Companion of the Order of the Star of India) and FRS (Fellow of the Royal Society).

Muniruddin

  • A master engraver who contributed to both British India and Independent India’s philatelic designs.
  • Renowned for iconic stamps like the 1/2 Anna, India's first Republic Day stamp, and the Mahatma Gandhi stamp.
  • Honored with the National Award for Best Stamp Designer in 1964.
